Speak the Beat

A metronome that counts aloud

How to use

Tap the green panel or press spacebar to start and stop.

Time Signature sets how many beats are in each bar. The first beat is always accented.

Subdivision adds quiet in-between clicks — 8th notes or 16th notes — so you can feel the subdivisions without counting them aloud.

Count Style controls what the voice says. All beats counts every beat by number. One only calls the downbeat only — useful at fast tempos. Silent lets the clicks do the work.

Click Tone changes the sound of the click. Try Bell for a chime, Beep for a classic electronic metronome, or Soft for quiet practice.

Tap HERE to Set Tempo — tap the button repeatedly in time and the BPM will lock to your tapping.

The Voice selector lets you choose who counts. Check English only to filter the list.

VOICE CALIBRATION

Every device has a small delay between when the app asks it to speak and when the voice actually comes out of the speaker. This is called TTS latency — and it varies from phone to phone.

The orange arrow shows where the click and flash land — that is the beat. The blue arrow shows where the voice is landing. In a perfect world they coincide.

Auto correction measures your device's lag over the first few beats and applies the correction silently. For most users this is all you need.

Manual correction lets you hear the difference yourself. Slide right and left until the voice lands where you want it relative to the beat. The number shows how many milliseconds of delay you are applying.

The Mute Click button silences the click tone so you can listen to the voice alone while calibrating.

The generated voice may lag behind the beat. This changes with each device. Automatic compensation is made, or you may apply the compensation for your device here, manually. The lag is measured in milliseconds.
